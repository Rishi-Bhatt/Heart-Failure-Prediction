\subsubsection{Longitudinal Prediction Evaluation}

We evaluated the system's longitudinal tracking and forecasting capabilities using a dataset of 150 patients with at least 3 years of follow-up data. The evaluation focused on:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Forecast Accuracy}: How well the system predicted future risk levels
    \item \textbf{Calibration}: Whether confidence intervals accurately reflected uncertainty
    \item \textbf{Intervention Impact}: Accuracy of predicted intervention effects
\end{itemize}

\begin{table}[ht]
  \vspace{2mm}
  \centering
  \resizebox{0.95\columnwidth}{!}{%
    \begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|}
      \hline
      \textbf{Forecast Horizon} & \textbf{RMSE} & \textbf{Calibration Error} & \textbf{AUC} \\
      \hline
      6 months & 0.082 & 0.031 & 0.89 \\
      1 year & 0.104 & 0.045 & 0.87 \\
      2 years & 0.138 & 0.062 & 0.84 \\
      \hline
    \end{tabular}%
  }
  \vspace{2mm}
  \caption{Longitudinal forecasting performance metrics}
  \label{tab:longitudinal-eval}
\end{table}

The results demonstrate that the system maintains strong predictive performance even for longer time horizons, with only moderate degradation in accuracy at the 2-year mark. The calibration error, which measures the difference between predicted confidence intervals and observed error distributions, remains low across all time horizons, indicating well-calibrated uncertainty estimates.
